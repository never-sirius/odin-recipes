Onion Soup

French onion soup, or soupe à l'oignon gratinée, is a classic French dish made with caramelized onions and beef stock or broth. The soup is topped with bread and cheese, then gratinéed to rich, gooey perfection. It's traditionally served in a ramekin or a ceramic crock with a handle.

Onion Soup Ingredients
    Half a cup unsalted butter
    Two tablespoons olive oil
    Four cups sliced onions
    Five cups beef broth
    Two tablespoons dry sherry
    One tablespoon dried thyme
    One pinch salt and pepper to taste
    Four slices of french bread
    Four slices of provolone cheese
    Two slices of swiss cheese, diced
    HAlf a cup grated Parmesan cheese
Instructions Gather all ingredients. Melt butter with olive oil in an 8-quart stock pot over medium heat. Add onions to butter and continually stir until tender and translucent. Do not brown the onions. Add beef broth, sherry, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper. Let simmer for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat the oven's broiler. Ladle soup into oven-safe serving bowls and place one slice of bread on top of each (bread may be broken into pieces if you prefer). Layer each slice of bread with a slice of provolone, 1/2 slice diced Swiss and 1 tablespoon Parmesan cheese. Place bowls on a cookie sheet and broil in the preheated oven until cheese bubbles and browns slightly, 2 to 3 minutes. Serve hot and enjoy!
